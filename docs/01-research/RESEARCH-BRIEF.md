## Top 5 Insights

### Insight 1 — The gap is demand without supply, not supply without demand
75% of Americans want to reduce sugar. The largest sugar-specific app has 1,600 Google Play ratings. Reframe (alcohol) has 3.2M downloads in a market 3–5× smaller. This is not a market that doesn't exist — it is a market that has never had a product worth using. The opportunity is supply-side, not demand-side.

*Sources: MARKET-RESEARCH.md §1.1, §3.3; COMPETITORS.md §Strategic Conclusions*

### Insight 2 — Every competitor fails at the only moment that matters
The craving window (15–20 minutes) is when the decision is made. Every app in the category either ignores it (trackers) or acknowledges it with a breathing GIF (panic buttons). No app talks to the user, knows their patterns, or tells them what to eat right now. The product that wins is the one that shows up at that moment with something genuinely useful.

*Sources: COMPETITORS.md §Gap 1; USER-PERSONAS.md §Cross-Persona Patterns; MARKET-RESEARCH.md §4.4*

### Insight 3 — The Reframe playbook is directly transferable
Reframe hit $10M ARR with a 160-day neuroscience curriculum + AI chatbot + community + multi-tier pricing. Every element maps to sugar: the curriculum (90 days, dopamine/cortisol framing), the AI (SOS session), the community (r/sugarfree equivalent), the pricing ($99/yr). The only layer to add is sugar-specific: food scanner, alternatives engine, environmental trigger navigation. The playbook is proven; the domain is new.

*Sources: COMPETITORS.md §4 Reframe; PRODUCT-BRIEF.md §1; MARKET-RESEARCH.md §7.2*

### Insight 4 — Cortisol is the insight that unlocks Stress Sarah
The primary persona's craving is not hunger — it is a cortisol response to workplace stress. Reframing the 3pm candy bowl moment as "your stress hormone is asking for fast glucose, not weakness" removes moral weight and creates a coachable moment. This reframe is scientifically accurate (DOMAIN-RESEARCH.md §1 — Cortisol definition) and is the AI's most powerful opening line in any SOS session. No competitor uses this framing.

*Sources: DOMAIN-RESEARCH.md §1 Cortisol, §4.3 SOS Framework; USER-PERSONAS.md §Persona 1*

### Insight 5 — 66 days, not 30 days, is the real milestone
The habit formation literature (Lally 2010) shows average habit automaticity at 66 days, range 18–254. Every competitor uses 30-day framing because it maps to social media challenges. A 90-day curriculum is differentiated, scientifically grounded, and has higher retention value (more subscription months). The 30-day mark should be celebrated as the midpoint, not the finish line. Critically: Lally also shows a single missed day does not break habit formation — the non-punitive streak design is not just good UX, it's what the science actually says.

*Sources: DOMAIN-RESEARCH.md §2 Source 3 (Lally 2010), §4.2 Curriculum; DOMAIN-RESEARCH.md §5 Key Conclusions*

## Risks

### Risk 1 — Low urgency ceiling (HIGH severity, MEDIUM probability)
Sugar does not carry the acute social stigma or medical urgency of alcohol, opioids, or obesity-as-diagnosed-condition. Users may feel "I should probably eat less sugar" rather than "I need help now." This reduces conversion from awareness to paying subscriber. Mitigation: lean into the Pre-Diabetic Paul segment (medical urgency is real) and the social challenge mechanic for Chloe (extrinsic motivation substitutes for urgency). Validate WTP with a $0 → $9.99 paywall test in the first 60 days post-launch.

### Risk 2 — AI quality is the entire moat (HIGH severity, MEDIUM probability)
The product's differentiation depends entirely on the SOS AI being genuinely better than a button and a GIF. If the AI gives generic, unhelpful responses, the entire value proposition collapses. This is an execution risk, not a market risk — but it is the single most critical build decision. Mitigation: invest in prompt engineering and session quality testing before launch; define success metrics for SOS sessions (did craving pass? did user rate it helpful?); use Claude API / GPT-4-class model, not a cheaper model.

*See CLAUDE.md — Claude API / OpenAI API are in the confirmed tech stack.*

### Risk 3 — Engagement cliff at Day 21 (MEDIUM severity, HIGH probability)
Research (Linardon 2020) shows engagement in behaviour-change apps drops sharply after week 3 without active personalisation. The curriculum content must escalate in personalisation after day 21, not stay static. Mitigation: trigger-based content delivery (AI adapts lessons to the user's logged patterns), social accountability features, and a re-engagement push strategy specifically designed for days 18–24.

### Risk 4 — Large player entry (HIGH severity, LOW probability near-term)
Noom, MyFitnessPal, or a well-funded new entrant could pivot to sugar in 12–18 months. Noom has the CBT infrastructure and user base; MyFitnessPal has the food database. Mitigation: the data moat (personalised trigger patterns and craving history) compounds with time — launch fast, acquire users, build the dataset. Community lock-in (social connections within the app) also raises switching costs. The window is open now; it will not stay open indefinitely.

### Risk 5 — Eating disorder liability (MEDIUM severity, LOW probability with mitigation)
Any app in the food/sugar space risks harmful engagement with users who have eating disorders (anorexia, orthorexia, bulimia). A single high-profile incident can trigger App Store removal or reputational damage. Mitigation: implement the onboarding screening, eating disorder resource referral, and no-calorie-display rules documented in DOMAIN-RESEARCH.md §3.4 before launch, not as a later addition.

## Hypotheses to Validate

### H1 — The SOS session is the activation event
**Hypothesis:** Users who complete one SOS session within their first 3 days of installation will have 2× the Day-30 retention of users who do not.
**How to test:** Measure SOS session completion rate in first 72 hours vs. Day-30 retention. If true: invest heavily in first-session prompting and onboarding flow that leads directly to SOS. If false: identify what actually drives early retention.

### H2 — Trigger prediction increases 7-day retention
**Hypothesis:** Users who receive a correctly-timed predictive craving alert ("In 30 minutes, you usually crave sugar") will show higher Day-7 retention than users on generic push notifications.
**How to test:** A/B test predictive alerts (requires ≥7 days of pattern data) vs. time-of-day generic reminders. Requires minimum 2 weeks of user data before trigger model is useful — test on cohorts who joined in week 1.

### H3 — Reduction mode converts better than quit mode
**Hypothesis:** Users who select "I want to reduce sugar by 50%" in onboarding will have higher 30-day retention than users who select "I want to quit sugar completely," because reduction is a more sustainable commitment.
**How to test:** Offer both goal modes in onboarding; track retention by mode. If true: position "reduce" as the default goal, not the fallback for people who can't commit.

### H4 — The food scanner is acquisition, not retention
**Hypothesis:** The scanner is a viral acquisition feature (shareable, discoverable) but does not materially affect Day-30 retention — users who rely primarily on the scanner will churn at the same rate as non-scanner users.
**How to test:** Segment retention by primary feature usage in first 7 days (scanner vs. SOS vs. curriculum). If true: invest in scanner for ASO/social virality but do not build the retention strategy around it.

### H5 — Pre-diabetic users have 2× higher LTV
**Hypothesis:** Users who indicate a medical reason for reducing sugar (pre-diabetes, doctor's recommendation) in onboarding will have higher conversion to paid and longer subscription duration than users with aesthetic or challenge motivations.
**How to test:** Tag onboarding motivation; track conversion rate and subscription length by motivation segment. If true: allocate B2B and healthcare marketing budget toward the Paul segment sooner.

## MVP Recommendations

### Build for MVP

| What | Why |
|------|-----|
| SOS AI session (5-step framework) | The entire value proposition; everything else is secondary |
| Predictive craving alerts (Day 7+) | Differentiator that no competitor has; requires only 7 days of pattern data |
| 30-day starter curriculum (Days 1–30 of 90) | Enough to validate engagement; extend to 90 days post-validation |
| "Eat this instead" engine (static v1) | #1 unmet need; static list is good enough to test; personalise in v2 |
| Non-punitive streak + slip recovery flow | Scientifically correct; retains users through the Day-21 cliff |
| Onboarding eating disorder screen + disclaimer | Non-negotiable before any public launch |
| Free tier with Day 1–7 curriculum + 3 SOS sessions | Acquisition without conversion pressure; let the product sell itself |

### Do NOT Build for MVP

| What | Why Not |
|------|---------|
| Food barcode scanner | Competitive disadvantage only if broken (Sugarfree's #1 complaint); requires large accurate database; defer to v2 |
| Community / social features | Cold-start problem; empty community is worse than no community; launch when you have ≥500 DAU |
| Human coaching tier | High marginal cost; unscalable; add only after $500K ARR validates demand |
| Vision Pro / Apple Watch | Premature platform expansion; focus on core iOS + Android |
| Full 90-day curriculum | Build 30 days; validate completion rate before investing in days 31–90 |
| Challenge mode (Chloe) | Valuable for acquisition but adds complexity; ship after core retention loop is proven |

### Monetisation for MVP

**Recommended launch model:**

| Tier | Price | Trigger |
|------|-------|---------|
| Free | $0 | Days 1–7 curriculum + 3 SOS sessions/month |
| Premium | $9.99/mo or $79.99/yr | Unlimited SOS + full curriculum + trigger prediction |
| Paywall placement | After first SOS session | User has experienced value before being asked to pay |

**Rationale:**
- Free tier must be genuinely useful (I Am Sober proof) — not a 7-day trial with a hard wall
- Paywall after the first SOS session is the highest-intent moment: the user just experienced the core value
- Annual plan at $79.99 is the primary revenue driver; monthly is for users who won't commit
- Do not offer lifetime pricing at launch — it caps LTV before you understand retention
- B2B / corporate wellness: do not build for MVP; approach 2–3 companies as pilot partners in month 3 if D30 retention ≥ 25%

## Verdict

# GO

**Score: 8.4 / 10**

### Justification

**The opportunity is real, the timing is right, and the playbook exists.**

The case for GO rests on three non-negotiable pillars:

**1. Structural market gap.** 75% of Americans want to reduce sugar; the best-performing sugar app has 1,600 ratings. This is not a niche — it is a mainstream health behaviour that the app market has failed to serve. Reframe proved the adjacent category (alcohol) could support $10M ARR and $12.5M in funding. Sugar's addressable market is meaningfully larger.

**2. Defensible differentiator.** The AI SOS session is both genuinely novel (no competitor does it) and deeply aligned with the actual job users need done (help in the moment of the craving, not retrospective logging). Trigger prediction adds a compounding data moat. Neither feature requires building proprietary AI — Claude/GPT-4 APIs make it technically viable at acceptable cost.

**3. Clear, validated playbook.** Reframe's formula — neuroscience curriculum + AI craving support + community + multi-tier pricing + outcome data — is directly transferable with one addition layer (food alternatives, scanner, environmental navigation). The 90-day curriculum maps precisely onto Lally (2010) habit formation science. The monetisation model has comparables at scale.

### Conditions on the GO

The verdict is GO, not STRONG GO, for three reasons requiring active management:

1. **Monetisation is unvalidated in this specific category.** Willingness to pay for sugar-specific help is inferred from adjacent markets, not proven. The first 60 days post-launch must include a rigorous WTP test. If Day-30 conversion to paid is below 3%, revisit the freemium structure.

2. **AI quality is the entire moat.** A bad SOS session is worse than no SOS session — it destroys the value proposition and accelerates churn. The AI must be built and tested to a high quality bar before any public launch, not shipped and iterated under real users.

3. **Launch by January 2027 at the latest.** The seasonality spike (New Year's resolutions, "no sugar January" TikTok wave) is the highest-leverage GTM moment. Missing it delays meaningful user acquisition by ~11 months. The window before a well-funded competitor enters is estimated at 12–18 months from now.

**If the team can build a genuinely good SOS AI and launch before January 2027: this is the right product, in the right market, at the right time.**
